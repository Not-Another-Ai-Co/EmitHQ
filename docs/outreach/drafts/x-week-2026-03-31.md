## Friday — What Happens When It Fails (thread, 3 tweets + image)

### Tweet 1

Your webhook endpoint goes down. Here's what should happen next:

Attempt 1: immediate.
Attempt 2: ~30s (jittered).
Attempts 3-8: exponential backoff over 29 hours.

No thundering herd. No hammering a dead server.

### Tweet 2

10 consecutive failures? Circuit breaker trips — endpoint auto-disabled, you get an operational webhook notification.

All 8 retries exhausted? Message goes to a dead-letter queue. One-click replay when you're ready.

Zero messages lost.

### Tweet 3

This is EmitHQ's default behavior. Every tier, including free.

Most "build it yourself" webhook systems skip circuit breakers and DLQs. You only realize you need them after losing messages in production.
